Patient Information
Definitions & Instructions

Rate symptom severity during the prior week up to and including the time of interview. Scores should reflect the average occurrence for the entire week.

OBSESSIONS are unwelcome and distressing ideas, thoughts, images or impulses that repeatedly enter your mind. They may seem to occur against your will. They may be repugnant to you, you may recognise them as senseless, and they may not fit your personality.

COMPULSIONS are behaviours or acts that you feel driven to perform although you may recognise them as senseless or excessive. At times, you may try to resist doing them but this may prove difficult. You may experience anxiety that does not diminish until the behaviour is completed.

2Interference Due to Obsessive Thoughts

3Distress Associated with Obsessive Thoughts

How much of an effort do you make to resist the obsessive thoughts? How often do you try to disregard or turn your attention away from these thoughts as they enter your mind?
5Degree of Control Over Obsessive Thoughts

11Insight into Obsessions and Compulsions
Do you think your concerns or behaviours are reasonable? What do you think would happen if you did not perform the compulsion(s)?
12Avoidance
Have you been avoiding doing anything, going any place, or being with anyone because of your obsessional thoughts or out of concern you will perform compulsions?
13Degree of Indecisiveness
Do you have trouble making decisions about little things that other people might not think twice about?
14Overvalued Sense of Responsibility
Do you feel very responsible for the consequences of your actions? Do you blame yourself for the outcome of events not completely in your control?
15Pervasive Slowness / Disturbance of Inertia
Do you have difficulty starting or finishing tasks? Do many routine activities take longer than they should?
16Pathological Doubting
After you complete an activity do you doubt whether you performed it correctly? Do you doubt whether you did it at all?
17Global Severity (CGI-adapted)
Interviewer's judgement of the overall severity of the patient's illness. Consider distress, symptoms observed, and functional impairment.
18Global Improvement (CGI-adapted)
Rate total overall improvement since the initial rating, whether or not it is due to treatment.
19Reliability
Rate the overall reliability of the rating scores obtained.

Disclaimer: The Y-BOCS is a clinician-rated semi-structured interview instrument developed by Goodman WK, Price LH, Rasmussen SA et al. (1989). Total score is derived from items 1–10 only. Items 11–19 are supplementary. This form is intended as a clinical aid and does not replace clinical judgement. Interpretation should be made in the context of a comprehensive psychiatric evaluation. A clinically meaningful treatment response is typically defined as ≥35% reduction from baseline Y-BOCS score.
