    How to Use This Tool

    How to Use This Tool

    Purpose: A structured clinical companion for ADHD assessments — not a replacement for clinical judgment, but a way to stay organised, capture everything, and leave with a complete document set ready to send.

    Overview & Philosophy

    What this does

    • Guides you through a full ADHD assessment in a logical order
    • Captures clinical data as you go and auto-generates outputs at the end
    • Produces a patient letter, titration plan, GP handover, educational handout, and specialist letters — all from the same session
    • Outputs a plain-text context block for Heidi, MedicalDirector, Best Practice or any AI scribe

    How to use it

    • Follow along during the consult — tap items as the patient describes them. The form follows the natural arc of a psychiatric assessment
    • Use after the consult — fill in from memory or notes, then generate all outputs at once
    • Hybrid — pre-fill demographic info before the patient joins, fill clinical sections during, finish outputs after
    • Nothing is compulsory. Fill what's relevant and leave the rest

    Data & privacy

    • Everything stays in your browser — no data is sent anywhere
    • The form auto-saves to local storage as you type
    • Use Reset (top right) to clear between patients
    Timing Guide — Staying Within 50–60 Minutes

    Suggested time allocation

    • 0–5 min — Presenting concerns, reason for referral, demographics
    • 5–10 min — Pre-screening review (ASRS, DIVA, collateral). Check what the GP has already done
    • 10–20 min — Developmental history. Childhood pattern, adolescence, adult presentation. Tick core symptoms as patient describes them
    • 20–30 min — Differential diagnoses, substance use, family history. These can move quickly with targeted questions
    • 30–35 min — Mental state examination, DSM criteria review (mostly auto-filled by this point)
    • 35–40 min — Medical history, cardiac screening, investigations, SafeScript
    • 40–50 min — Treatment rationale, medication selection, titration plan, follow-up
    • 50–60 min — Psychoeducation discussion, handout selection, patient letter review, wrap-up

    Tips to stay on time

    • The ? buttons next to each item give suggested interview questions — use these to prompt without losing structure
    • Differentials default to Nil — only change ones that are relevant
    • DSM criteria and diagnosis auto-complete as you fill in the developmental section
    • Skip the titration builder if continuing an existing plan — just note it in the authority/Rx box
    Psych Hx Tab

    Presenting Concerns

    • Fill patient name, age, gender and referring GP at the top — these flow into all generated letters
    • Tap difficulties as the patient describes them — no need to type

    Pre-screening & Collateral

    • Record ASRS and DIVA outcomes with one tap — include whether patient or clinician administered
    • Collateral section covers observer ASRS, GP referral, school reports, psych reports, Vanderbilt scales
    • Items are deselectable — tap again to clear

    Developmental History — Core Symptoms

    • The ? button on each item reveals interview prompts tailored to that criterion
    • Tick the 18 DSM-5-TR core symptoms as the patient describes them — inattention and H/I tracked separately
    • Once 5+ symptoms are ticked in either domain, Criterion A auto-checks
    • Childhood, cross-situational and impairment items auto-fill Criteria B, C and D

    Differential Diagnoses

    • All default to Nil — only change what's relevant
    • Nil / Past / Current for each condition
    • Flagged conditions (bipolar, schizophrenia, suicidality, eating disorders, ABI, substance-induced) turn red and trigger a flag when marked Current
    • ? button shows key differential questions for each condition
    • Criterion E (not better explained) auto-unchecks if any Current differential is set — you can manually re-check it if you disagree

    Auto-DSM & Diagnosis

    • DSM criteria auto-check as you fill the developmental section
    • Diagnosis and subtype auto-set when all 5 criteria are met
    • You can override any auto-selection manually — it sticks
    • Deselect All resets manual overrides so auto-logic can take over again
    Medical Hx Tab

    Cardiac Screening

    • Each item has a ? button with a clinical note explaining prescribing implications
    • Flagged items (MAOI, hypertension, structural heart disease etc.) trigger red flags in the Plan tab

    SafeScript / RTPM

    • Record SafeScript status — "Not checked" triggers a gap in the Plan tab as a reminder
    • NSW RTPM required before prescribing stimulants

    Tab badge

    • A red number appears on the Medical Hx tab when there are active flags — tells you at a glance if anything needs attention without switching tabs
    Plan Tab — Flags, Gaps & Treatment Rationale

    Flags & Gaps panels

    • Flags — safety and clinical alerts (suicidality, cardiac concerns, SafeScript not checked, current substances, billing conflicts)
    • Gaps — outstanding items before the assessment is complete (MSE not done, no follow-up set, DSM criteria incomplete)
    • Both update live as you fill in the form

    Treatment Rationale grid

    • Tap through: pregnancy/breastfeeding status → contraindications → first/second/third line → duration → medication class
    • Selecting a line automatically dims irrelevant options (e.g. First-line dims the non-stimulant and third-line rows)
    • Auto-generates a rationale sentence in the notes box below

    Psychoeducation & Handout generator

    • Tick the topics discussed during the consult — they appear in the patient handout
    • Topics include: what ADHD is, how medications work, side effects, stimulant comparisons, sleep, emotional regulation, study tips, driving, phone use, products and resources
    • The handout is generated as a formatted section within the patient document — ready to print or send
    • Tick count shown in the card header so you can see at a glance how much has been covered
    Titration Plan Builder

    Using presets

    • Tap any protocol button to load it — Dexamphetamine, Ritalin IR/LA, Concerta, Vyvanse, Atomoxetine, Guanfacine, Clonidine
    • Multiple medications can be loaded and displayed together
    • Each step shows duration, timing and dose — all editable inline

    Building manually

    • Click "+ Add medication manually" — select the medication from a dropdown (no typing required)
    • Add, remove or reorder steps and doses within each medication block
    • Mark a step as "Ongoing" for the maintenance phase
    • Switch medication mid-plan using the dropdown in the block header

    Outputs

    • eScript text — formatted day-by-day dosing ready to copy into an eScript or pharmacy label
    • Rich plan — formatted table with authority text, step labels, dosing, medication info, safety scenarios and sign-off
    • Day-by-day calendar — checkboxes for each day with the 20-day rule highlighted
    Outputs Tab — Documents & Letters

    Heidi / AI context output

    • Plain text summary of everything entered — structured by section
    • Paste directly into Heidi's context field, Dragon notes, or any AI scribe
    • Reminds you to include detail you might forget or prefer not to say aloud in front of the patient — medication authority numbers, screening results, collateral findings, safety flags
    • Useful for dictating letters after the consult using a scribe that has full context

    Patient document builder

    • Toggle sections on/off — cover letter, medication plan, medication info, monitoring, non-pharmacological recommendations, if-concerns table, educational handout
    • Add optional extras: medical certificate (with dates), travel letter, medication possession letter, workplace adjustments, study adjustments, driving/machinery letter
    • Preview updates live. Download as .docx or .rtf, print to PDF, or copy for Best Practice

    Cover letter

    • Written in second person to the patient — summarises diagnosis, what follows in the document, medication, next steps and safety netting
    • Automatically populates from the clinical data you entered — diagnosis, subtype, medications, follow-up appointments, monitoring requirements

    Specialist letters

    • Travel — confirms diagnosis and prescription, explains false positives on drug screening, requests uninterrupted medication access
    • Medication possession — detailed drug screening explanation including GC-MS/LC-MS confirmatory testing
    • Workplace adjustments — confirms diagnosis, explains occupational impact, provides example adjustments
    • Study adjustments — academic context, extended time, separate venue, recorded lectures, assistive technology
    • Driving / machinery — explicitly confirms fitness to drive and operate machinery on current medication, cites evidence base
    • Medical certificate — confirms attendance and unfitness period, preserves confidentiality
    Shortcuts & Tips

    General

    • ? buttons — clinical prompts and interview questions for any item, without cluttering the main view
    • Select all / Deselect all — on any checklist section for quick bulk actions
    • All nil — sets every differential or substance to Nil in one tap
    • Tab badges — red numbers on Psych Hx and Medical Hx tabs show flag count without switching tabs
    • Prescriber Guide button — state-by-state stimulant authority requirements, dose limits, RTPM and UDS rules, available anywhere in the form

    Filling efficiently

    • Psychiatrist name is saved between sessions — you only need to enter it once
    • Date auto-fills to today
    • MBS item and 291/92435 eligibility check sit together — flag triggers automatically if you bill 291/92435 and mark Yes
    • Differential buttons are deselectable — tap again to undo
    • DSM criteria and diagnosis update automatically as you fill in the developmental section — you usually don't need to touch them

    Document generation

    • Refresh the document preview at any point during the consult to see how it's taking shape
    • GP handover letter generates from the Follow-Up section — tick the checkbox, enter the GP name, and it appears in the document automatically
    • All letters use the patient's name, diagnosis, medication and date from the form — no manual editing needed for most cases

    PBS Authority — How to Get It

    Go to PRODA → HPOS → My Providers → Authorities → Request Authority. You need your authority script authorisation number (next to your prescriber number on eScripts). You need a new authority for every new script, even if you're represcribing a script with previous authorisation.

    In the Authority Request, enter the total tablets per day and the total repeats you'll actually prescribe. Vyvanse needs individual PRODA authority for each capsule strength.

    For patients on 5+ tablets/day of dex IR or methylphenidate IR, the 20-day rule means they may run out. Enter 200 tablets (not the default 100) to get authority for 2 bottles. 200 is the maximum for methylphenidate; dex has no legal limit but be careful above 200.

    Private scripts: You can write a private script for any of these medications provided you have state pre-approval or individual state authorisation — regardless of whether the medication is on the PBS. PBS authority only determines whether the patient gets it subsidised. For example, if you obtain state approval to prescribe Vyvanse for binge eating disorder, you must write a private script because Vyvanse is only PBS-listed for ADHD.
