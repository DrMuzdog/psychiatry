MADRS Instructions

Rate each item based on the past week. The rating should be based on a clinical interview, moving from broadly phrased questions about symptoms to more detailed ones. The rater must decide whether the rating lies on defined scale steps (0, 2, 4, 6) or between (1, 3, 5).

HAM-A Instructions

Clinician-rated. Rate each of the 14 items on the severity experienced by the patient over the past week (or since last visit). Each item is scored on a 0–4 scale: 0 = not present, 1 = mild, 2 = moderate, 3 = severe, 4 = very severe / incapacitating. Total range 0–56.

HAM-A Plain Text Summary

Reference: Hamilton M. The assessment of anxiety states by rating. Br J Med Psychol. 1959;32:50-5. Commonly used severity thresholds: <17 mild; 18–24 mild-to-moderate; 25–30 moderate-to-severe; >30 severe. A cut-point of ≥14 is often used to define clinically significant anxiety.
YMRS Instructions

Clinician-rated. Rate each of the 11 items based on the patient's subjective report and behavior observed during the interview (past 48 hours). Items 5, 6, and 9 are double-weighted (scored 0–8) to reflect the greater difficulty eliciting cooperation for those items in severely ill patients. Other items are 0–4. Total range 0–60.

PCL-5 Plain Text Summary

Reference: Weathers FW, Litz BT, Keane TM, Palmieri PA, Marx BP, Schnurr PP. The PTSD Checklist for DSM-5 (PCL-5). National Center for PTSD, 2013. A cut-off of ≥31–33 has been suggested as indicating probable PTSD. Provisional DSM-5 PTSD diagnosis requires ≥1 item from cluster B (1–5), ≥1 from C (6–7), ≥2 from D (8–14), and ≥2 from E (15–20), each rated ≥2.

MSI-BPD Instructions

Clinician-administered screening instrument for borderline personality disorder. 10 yes/no items. A score of ≥7 is the suggested cut-off for a positive screen — a positive result indicates that a full diagnostic assessment for BPD is warranted, not a diagnosis.

CGI Instructions

Clinician Global Impression. Two independent 7-point items — Severity (CGI-S, rated at every visit) and Improvement (CGI-I, rated from the second visit onward, anchored to the initial assessment). Either or both can be left blank.

CGI-S — Severity of Illness

Considering your total clinical experience with this particular population, how mentally ill is the patient at this time?

CGI-I — Global Improvement

CGI Plain Text Summary

Reference: Guy W. ECDEU Assessment Manual for Psychopharmacology, Revised. NIMH, 1976. Single-item global ratings; widely used in research and clinical practice alongside symptom-specific scales.
