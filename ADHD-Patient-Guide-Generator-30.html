Prescribing authorities for stimulants in Australia

There are two separate authorities when prescribing ADHD stimulants: state or territory authority to prescribe and PBS authority for subsidy. Each jurisdiction has its own framework. For routine ADHD stimulant prescribing in eligible non-drug-dependent patients, psychiatrists prescribe under a class authority, standing approval, or equivalent — not an individual patient approval.

State-by-state
  • NSW: Write CA2023 on every stimulant script being dispensed at an NSW pharmacy, regardless of where you or the patient are based.
  • VIC: Psychiatrists can prescribe routine ADHD stimulants without a permit. Individual S8 permits handled via SafeScript if required.
  • QLD: Psychiatrists have standing approval within Queensland.
  • WA: Interstate specialists can prescribe for ADHD without a WA-specific permit.
  • SA: If the S8 is intended for more than 2 months, obtain individual patient authority via SA SafeScript.
  • ACT: From 11 February 2026, prescribe under the ACT Standing CHO Approval if the case fits; otherwise obtain individual CHO approval.
  • NT: Apply for authority via NT Government Health. Approval lasts 2 years.
  • TAS: Check current state requirements.

Individual patient authority — Required for non-ADHD indications, drug dependence, or above-standard doses. Log in to the relevant state RTPM system (SafeScript NSW, SafeScript VIC, QScript QLD, ScriptCheckWA) and use the authority request function. May take 1–2 days.

PBS authority — Separate from state authority. Obtained via PRODA/HPOS. Without it the patient pays private price. PBS stimulant authority workflow changed from 1 June 2025 — use the current online process.

High-dose dexamfetamine or methylphenidate IR (≥5 tablets/day): PRODA authority required to keep cost capped. When submitting, enter 200 tablets (not the default 100) to account for the higher daily quantity.

Special indications
  • Narcolepsy (dexamfetamine): Obtain individual patient authority through SafeScript — the NSW ADHD class authority will be rejected for narcolepsy.
  • Binge eating disorder (Vyvanse): Write a private script — PBS approval is not available for BED. Attempting PBS approval will be rejected.
Cost without PBS authority
  • Dexamfetamine: Relatively inexpensive privately. PBS authority still recommended but cost impact is lower.
  • Lisdexamfetamine (Vyvanse): ~$50–$100/pack. PRODA authority strongly recommended.
  • Methylphenidate (Ritalin IR/LA, Concerta): Moderate–high cost without authority. Concerta requires PBS authority and diagnosis must have been established before age 18.
  • Atomoxetine (Strattera): Streamline code 7876 (initiation), 7890 (continuation). Without authority ~$75–$100/pack.
  • Guanfacine (Intuniv): PRODA authority recommended. Without authority ~$70–$150/pack.
  • Clonidine (Catapres): Not PBS-listed for ADHD — private script only. No authority required within standard quantities.
