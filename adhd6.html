Set the frame. Confirm consent. Hear the patient's story before you reach for criteria.

    PrePre-screening & collateral

    Validated tools, prior reports, and informant accounts. Cover before you start the formal DSM-5 review.

      ASymptoms · ≥5 inattention or ≥5 hyperactive/impulsive

      Ask broadly — examples, not yes/no. Look for the pattern, not the checklist.

      Inattention
        Hyperactivity / impulsivity
          BSeveral symptoms present before age 12

          Establish a continuous thread from childhood. Ask for examples, then keep asking "what else?"

            CSymptoms in ≥2 settings

            Don't accept "just at work." Get specific examples in each setting.

              DClear functional impairment

              Impairment is the diagnosis. Be specific — what is harder than it should be, and what is the cost?

                ENot better explained by another condition

                For each differential — 2–3 differentiating questions, then a brief note on how to discern from ADHD. Click any line to expand.

                              Typical follow-up sequence
                              • 1–2 weeks: phone safety check after initiation
                              • 4–6 weeks: first formal titration review (BP, HR, side effects, response)
                              • 3 months: stabilisation review once on ongoing dose
                              • 3–4 months: handover letter drafted if stable
                              • 4–6 months: first GP-led review under shared care
                              • Annually: psychiatric review for stable regime
