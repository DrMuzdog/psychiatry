    PrePre-screening & collateral

    Validated tools, prior reports, and informant accounts. Cover before you start the formal DSM-5 review.

      ASymptoms · ≥5 inattention or ≥5 hyperactive/impulsive

      Ask broadly — examples, not yes/no. Look for the pattern, not the checklist.

        Hyperactivity / impulsivity
          BSeveral symptoms present before age 12

          Establish a continuous thread from childhood. Ask for examples, then keep asking "what else?"

            CSymptoms in ≥2 settings

            Don't accept "just at work." Get specific examples in each setting.

              DClear functional impairment

              Impairment is the diagnosis. Be specific — what is harder than it should be, and what is the cost?

                ENot better explained by another condition

                Screen each differential briefly. Comorbidity is the rule, not the exception.

                  ·Substance use

                  Screen every drug class. Don't skip caffeine, nicotine, or prescribed sedatives. Current vs past matters for safety.

                    ·Cardiac screen

                    Stimulants are cardiovascular drugs. Any red flag here means defer and refer.

                      ·Medical history

                      Broader medical context — what could complicate stimulant treatment, or what could be the real explanation.

                        ·Current medications & interactions

                        Full medication reconciliation — including OTC, supplements, herbals. Specific interactions to clear before stimulant.

                          ·Family psychiatric history

                          ADHD is highly heritable. Family history of bipolar or psychosis changes the risk–benefit calculus.
