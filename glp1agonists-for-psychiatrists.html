GLP-1 Agonists for Psychiatrists Australian Prescribing Guide

Purpose. A practical, evidence-based reference for Australian psychiatrists prescribing GLP-1 receptor agonists (semaglutide, liraglutide) and dual GLP-1/GIP receptor agonists (tirzepatide). Covers pharmacology, eligibility, safety review, prescribing protocols, the "click method" for selecting agents, expected weight loss, and the emerging mental-health evidence (addiction, depression, antipsychotic-induced weight gain, cognition).

Why psychiatrists. Patients with severe mental illness die 15–25 years earlier than the general population, predominantly from cardiometabolic disease. Antipsychotics, mood stabilisers and many antidepressants drive weight gain. GLP-1 agonists are now the most effective non-surgical intervention for obesity and are reshaping practice. Beyond weight, signals are emerging across alcohol use disorder, substance use, depression, and neurodegeneration. Psychiatrists need to be confident readers, prescribers, and stewards of these agents.

Disclaimer. Not medical advice. Clinical decisions are the prescriber's responsibility. Verify dosing, PBS listing, and TGA-approved indications against current sources (TGA, PBS, eTG, the relevant product information) before prescribing. Information accurate to publication date; this is a rapidly evolving field.
